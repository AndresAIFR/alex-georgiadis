About

Where guest experience
meets green innovation

With over twenty years dedicated to the art of hospitality, Alexandros Georgiadis brings a rare combination of operational mastery and environmental vision to every space he inhabits. From fine dining floors to corporate popup kitchens, his career has been defined by one principle: every guest interaction is an opportunity to create something memorable.

His journey took a distinctive turn at the University of Maryland, where he merged his hospitality expertise with environmental science—leading the design of an indoor living wall at Cole Field House that transformed how we think about air quality in shared spaces.

Today, as part of the Fooda team in the DC-Baltimore corridor, Alexandros orchestrates corporate dining experiences that connect local restaurant culture with workplace communities, serving thousands of professionals through innovative popup restaurant programs.

Signature Project

The Colefield House
Indoor Living Wall

A pioneering green infrastructure project at the University of Maryland that reimagines indoor air quality through nature. This living wall system uses carefully selected plant species to naturally filter and purify air in the Cole Field House recreation space—proving that sustainability and human comfort can coexist beautifully.

🌿

Natural Air Filtration

Spider plants, philodendrons, and other species selected for their proven ability to remove volatile organic compounds and improve indoor air quality.

💧

Water Recapture System

Engineered closed-loop irrigation that recycles water through the wall, minimizing waste while keeping plants thriving year-round.

🏗

Scalable Design

Built as a proof-of-concept for integrating biophilic systems into large recreational and commercial facilities at institutional scale.

🧬

Research-Backed

Every species and system component selected through rigorous environmental science research, with measurable impact on air quality metrics.
